Review

Metallica's '...And Justice for All' stands as a monumental and unapologetically heavy masterpiece in the world of thrash metal. Released in 1988, this album is a relentless sonic assault that combines intricate songwriting with technically impressive musicianship. The title track alone is a near 10-minute progressive metal epic, showcasing the band's ability to craft intricate arrangements while maintaining their signature aggression.

Following the unfortunate demise of Metallica's bassist, Cliff Burton, in 1986, the band took a bizarre approach to the mix of their next album - completely silencing the bass track of their new bassist, Jason Newsted.

While the lack of any sort of bassline in the album is a sore spot for many fans, this album remains a classic for its searing guitar work, relentless drumming, and James Hetfield's raw, powerful vocals. Tracks like 'One' and 'Blackened' exemplify Metallica's ability to blend melody with unrelenting intensity.


8.5/10
